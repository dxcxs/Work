About Me

A bit about who I am.

Background

I grew up in Nakhon Pathom and got into computers the way most people my age did — breaking things and figuring out how to fix them. I spent a lot of time in secondary school making simple websites for fun, not for any class. That habit stuck, and in 2024 I enrolled in Computer Science at NPRU to make it more than a hobby.

Where I want to go

I want to work on real products with people who take craft seriously. Short term that means an internship where I can contribute something useful and learn how professional teams actually ship software. Longer term I am interested in full-stack development and eventually infrastructure — understanding the whole system, not just the surface.

What I think about

A lot of what I read and experiment with comes back to one question: who gets left out when we build things carelessly? Screen reader users, people on slow connections, people using a phone with a cracked screen — they are all real users. I try to keep that in mind even when nobody is grading me on it.

Outside of code I play chess, tinker with Raspberry Pi projects, and occasionally help classmates debug things at midnight before a deadline.
